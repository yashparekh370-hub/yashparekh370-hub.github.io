Longer Writings

These are not quick reads. They were written during pauses, not rushes.

On Attachment & Letting Go

March 2025

Attachment rarely feels heavy in the beginning. It becomes weight only when it starts deciding your inner weather.

We often confuse attachment with care. Care is spacious. Attachment is anxious. One allows movement, the other demands certainty.

Letting go is not an act of rejection. It is the quiet decision to stop gripping what no longer grows with you.

Discipline During Low Phases

February 2025

Motivation is loud when life is kind. Discipline speaks only when nothing feels rewarding.

Low phases distort time. Days feel heavier, effort feels invisible, and progress feels absent. Discipline is what keeps structure intact when emotions collapse.

You don’t need intensity here. You need repetition — small, boring, uncelebrated repetition.

Learning to Sit With Discomfort

January 2025

Most discomfort is not danger. It is simply unfamiliar truth asking to be acknowledged.

We rush to label, fix, or escape discomfort. Sitting with it feels unproductive, even threatening.

But presence is not passive. It is the skill of staying without demanding resolution.
